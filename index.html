Adidas Trionda contact-detection simulator

This page models the key ideas behind the 2026 World Cup ball’s contact sensing with a sidewall-mounted IMU, a live pulse trace, battery behavior, and an offside scenario inspired by the Croatia vs. Portugal moment. It is an educational simulation grounded in the real architecture rather than a generic touch-switch model.

Beyond the contact type itself, the trace also reacts to environmental conditions, the ball's own physical state, and outside interference — use the controls below to see how each factor reshapes what the sensor reports.

Trionda vs. Al Rihla

The Adidas Trionda system is meant to be understood as a ball-internal inertial sensing system rather than a generic pressure or contact switch. Public descriptions point to a collaboration between FIFA and Kinexon, and it differs from the 2022 Al Rihla, which used a more centrally positioned sensor concept.

Key contrast:
  • Trionda: side-panel / sidewall-mounted inertial sensor approach
  • Al Rihla: center-mounted sensor architecture
  • Why it matters: the sidewall placement helps capture sharper, more localized inertial events
The simulation also highlights why the match officials and broadcast teams can be interested in the pulse trace: it helps separate a real touch from a tiny signal that could be mistaken for ambient noise.
